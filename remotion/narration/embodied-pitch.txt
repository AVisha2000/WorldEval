An agent can describe a plan perfectly and still fail when the plan has physical dependencies. If a car needs fuel, walking to the petrol station leaves the car behind. WorldArena measures whether an AI can act through those dependencies, not just explain them.

WorldArena is a deterministic, embodied agent evaluation arena. A model controls one operator through bounded movement, look, and interaction inputs. Godot, not the model, owns physics, collision, resources, and the outcome.

The solo curriculum makes that concrete. First, orient to a visible beacon. Then collect and deliver a resource. In Construction, an agent finds material, gathers it, carries it to a relay, deposits it, reaches a build pad, and holds the build action until a barricade is complete.

At each decision boundary, the agent receives a participant-scoped camera frame and visible state. It submits a bounded controller action, and fixed-tick Godot simulation resolves the consequence. This is not teleporting between screenshots.

That boundary also makes outcomes inspectable. Spectator state, credentials, and raw provider material stay out of public replays. A malformed or timed-out response becomes recorded no-input, not a hidden repair. Episodes produce receipts, checkpoints, evaluation data, and replay evidence that can be verified offline.

After solo control, WorldArena runs a mirrored two-leg duel. Both entrants use identical bodies and controls, then swap sides. We evaluate task completion, valid actions, interaction receipts, timeout behaviour, and replay integrity, not just a final win screen.

We built WorldArena with Codex and GPT-5.6. Codex accelerated the Godot authority, FastAPI boundaries, dashboard, tests, and this production workflow. GPT-5.6 is integrated through the OpenAI controller adapter for live participant-scoped runs. The gameplay shown here is a deterministic local demo, so it is reproducible without keys or network calls.

WorldArena makes agent behavior visible, fair, and replayable, so developers can evaluate what an AI does, not only what it says.
